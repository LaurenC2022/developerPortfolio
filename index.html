About Me

I like software development, because there are an array of career paths within one industry. Frontend developers have the opportunity to work closely with the design team. Creativity and creative people are fun. Working on technical problems is another avenue to explore as a frontend developer. I enjoy working on technical problems. Lastly, detailed consideration for the user is another area of frontend development just to name a few. In my undergraduate studies and work as a middle school math teacher, I learned there is need for accessibility in our diverse communities. I have interests in all of these specialities within frontend development. For these reasons - art, math and consideration of users - I choose to work in frontend development.


My career goals include: gaining experience in frontend website development, and earning a masters in computer science. To prepare for work as a developer, I am completing website development coursework. To read about the high quality learning materials I am using, please follow this link to a Notion website with the details of my learning plan:Professional Development - Frontend Software Engineer Learning Plan

I plan to be employed as a frontend developer by the end of 2023, and move on to my next goal: gaining a good foundation in computer science.

Over the next 2 to 3 years, I will be completing bridge coursework in undergraduate computer science studies, remotely. Then I would like to start applying to masters degree programs in computer science or software engineering.

Professionally, I want to be highly trained in building functional, secure, and accessible software with architecture for scalability. I am intersted now in learning Jamstack, though my goal is to learn full stack development. My goal is to be a frontend engineer and have full stack skills, so I can be a valuable team member in a variety of projects.
